# Competitor Guide

This guide covers how to find, register for, and participate in ballroom dance competitions on World of Floorcraft.

## Finding Competitions

Browse the **Competitions** page to see all available competitions. Use the tabs to filter:

| Tab | What You'll See |
|-----|----------------|
| **All** | Every competition on the platform |
| **Upcoming** | Competitions that are advertised or accepting entries |
| **Running** | Competitions currently in progress |
| **Finished** | Completed competitions with results |

Click any competition to view its details — venue, description, rules, and pricing.

---

## Registering for a Competition

When a competition is in **Accepting Entries** status, you'll see a **Register** button on the competition page.

### Registration Steps

1. **Click Register** on the competition page
2. **Enter your partner's username** (optional — you can register solo if still looking for a partner)
3. **Select your organization** (optional — you can enter unaffiliated)
4. Confirm registration

When you register with a partner, both of you get linked registration records. Either partner can manage entries afterward.

### After Registering

Your registration page shows:
- **Registration status** (registered, checked in, or cancelled)
- **Competitor number** (assigned by the organizer before the competition)
- **Entries** — the events you're competing in
- **Payment** — amount owed and total paid

---

## Entering Events

After registering, add yourself to events:

1. Click **Add Entries** on your registration page
2. Browse the event list — each event shows its style, level, and type (single/multi-dance)
3. Check the events you want to enter
4. Click **Add** to confirm

Events you've already entered show a checkmark and can't be selected again. To remove an entry, click **Remove** next to it.

You can also **scratch** an entry (a soft withdrawal that's reversible on comp day), which is different from removing it entirely.

---

## Finding a Partner (TBA)

If you need a partner for specific events, use the **Partner Finder (TBA)** page:

1. Navigate to the competition's TBA page
2. **Browse existing listings** — filter by style, level, and role
3. **Post your own listing** if you don't see a match:
   - Select the style and level you're looking for
   - Specify whether you need a leader or follower
   - Add optional notes (experience level, scheduling preferences, etc.)

TBA listings are public — anyone can browse them. Once you find a partner, you can delete your listing.

---

## Team Match

The **Team Match** page is where you can submit ideas for team match events (org-vs-org fun events). Write your suggestions in the text box and submit. Your submissions are visible to competition staff.

---

## After Entries Close

Once the competition moves to **Entries Closed** status, you can no longer add or remove entries directly. Instead, use the **Add/Drop Form** to request late changes:

1. Navigate to the competition's Add/Drop page
2. Click **New Request**
3. Select whether you want to **add** or **drop** an event
4. Choose the event
5. Optionally explain your reason
6. Submit the request

Your request will be reviewed by competition staff:
- **Pending** — waiting for review
- **Approved** — the change has been applied
- **Rejected** — the request was denied (the organizer may provide a reason)

Either partner in a couple can submit add/drop requests.

---

## Viewing Entries

The **Entries** page shows all registered couples grouped by event. This is public — anyone can see who's competing in what.

Each event shows:
- Event name, style, level
- Number of entries
- List of couples (leader & follower names)
- Organization affiliation (if any)

---

## Viewing Results

After the competition, results are published on the **Results** page.

Click any event to expand it and see:
- **Placements** — final rankings with couple names
- **Tabulation** — the detailed scoring breakdown (when available)

Top 3 placements are highlighted. Results are published per-event as the scrutineer reviews and approves them.

---

## Payment

Depending on how the organizer set up the competition:

- **Flat fee** — one price covers all your event entries
- **Per event** — each event has its own price; your total updates as you add/remove entries

Payment methods:
- **Online** — if the organizer has connected Stripe, you can pay by card
- **At the door** — pay cash, check, or other methods at the registration table

Your registration page shows your current balance (amount owed vs. paid).

---

## Competition Day

On comp day:
- Check in at the **registration table** — staff will mark you as checked in
- Your **competitor number** identifies your couple on the floor
- Watch the **schedule** for when your events are up
- If you need to withdraw from an event on comp day, talk to the **deck captain** — they can scratch your entry (reversible)

---

## Tips

- **Register early** — some competitions have limited spots
- **Use the TBA finder** if you don't have a partner yet — post your listing as soon as registration opens
- **Check the entries page** before the competition to see who else is competing
- **Submit add/drop requests promptly** after entries close — requests that don't affect round structure are usually approved quickly
- **Keep your profile up to date** — your display name and username are how your partner and other competitors find you
